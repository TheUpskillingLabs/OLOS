# The Upskilling Labs Hosts a Climate Hackathon on Capitol Hill — May 16

Press Release

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

May 8, 2026

DC Civic Learning Community Hosts One-Day Climate Hackathon on Capitol Hill — May 16

Participants tackle real energy and environmental challenges, build working prototypes, and leave with a plan to test them in the world — no technical background required.

WASHINGTON, D.C. — On Saturday, May 16, 2026, The Upskilling Labs — a DC-based AI learning nonprofit — will host the Idea to Prototype: Climate & Energy Hackathon , a one-day event where participants work in small research teams to address real environmental challenges and walk away with a working prototype and a plan to test it in the world. The event runs from 9:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. at St. Mark's Episcopal Church on Capitol Hill (301 A St SE, Washington, DC 20003).

The hackathon is open to all experience levels and is free to attend. Seats are limited.

### A Real On-Ramp for Beginners — Not Just a Day for Experts

What sets this event apart is a structured, beginner-friendly morning track. Newcomers spend the day in hands-on workshops covering design thinking, AI prompting techniques, and rapid prototyping — led by practitioners, not lecturers.

After lunch, newcomers join the existing research groups — called Pods — as fresh eyes and then resume workshops or build along with Pods. By 3:30 p.m., everyone presents what they built.

### The Pods: Four Real Climate Problems. Four Teams.

Each Pod is a focused research team working on a specific environmental challenge. The four problems being tackled on May 16th are:

•  Carbon Footprint: Making the carbon cost of everyday digital activity visible and understandable to general audiences.

•  Clean Energy: Building a navigator that helps households and community organizations find and access clean energy programs.

•  Recycling: Cutting through the confusion of local recycling rules with a tool that gives clear, actionable guidance.

•  Sustainable Travel: Giving consumers the tools to make lower-impact travel choices that the current industry structure makes unnecessarily difficult.

### About the Event

The hackathon builds on The Upskilling Labs' quarterly[Build Cycle program](https://theupskillinglabs.org/programming/energyandclimate), in which participants form Pods around real problems and spend twelve weeks researching, developing and testing AI-enabled solutions together.

What sets it apart: The hackathon is not just a one-day event but there's a genuine on-ramp for beginners and learners. Newcomers join in workshops on AI prompting, design thinking, and rapid prototyping, then join the Pods that have been incubating before and during the event. Experienced builders and first-timers end up working side by side- with many continuing iteration after the event.

The day wraps with prototype presentations from all tracks, followed by a happy hour nearby.
